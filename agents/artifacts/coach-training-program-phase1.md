# COACH — Phase 1 Training Program (Foundation)

Foundation block: 3x/week full body, initial progressive overload via double progression, ~10 sets/muscle/week starting volume for priority muscles. Source: knowledge/training-knowledge-base.md section 6 (Schoenfeld et al. 2016/2017: frequency and volume dose-response; Barbalho et al. 2019: 5-10 sets effective in women; Refalo et al. 2022: 1-3 RIR similar hypertrophy to failure; Plotkin et al. 2023: direct hip abduction work required for gluteus medius).

## Progression Rules

- Double progression: work within the prescribed rep range; when the top of the range is reached on ALL sets with good form, increase the load
- Load increments: +1 kg per dumbbell, +1.25 kg per side on barbell lifts, +2.5 kg on lower-body lifts and machines (smallest available increment)
- After increasing the load, drop back to the bottom of the rep range and build up again
- If micro-plates are unavailable, progress by reps only until +2.5 kg represents 10% or less of the working weight
- Compounds at RPE 7 (3 reps in reserve); isolation and abduction work at RPE 7-8
- Rate every set in the training log — logged sets drive next week's targets

## Recovery Protocol

- At least one full rest day between sessions (Monday / Wednesday / Friday)
- Sleep 7-9 hours; recovery is where adaptation happens
- Walking on rest days is encouraged; no additional structured training
- Deload every 6 weeks: same exercises, ~half the sets, loads at RPE 5-6
- Joint pain persisting beyond one week triggers a PHYSICIAN referral

## Phase Transition Criteria

- At least 14 weeks completed on Phase 1 AND a stall on 2 or more compound lifts for 3 or more sessions after a deload attempt
- OR 16 weeks of Phase 1 completed
- OR more training frequency requested while all lifts are still progressing

## Session Notes

Phase 1 builds the base: establish the main lifts, learn to gauge RPE honestly, and add load gradually through double progression — muscle grows on the small weekly increments, not on heroic single sessions. Expected rate of muscle gain is roughly 0.23-0.45 kg per month in year one: gradual and fully controllable through training and intake. Weight on the bar matters less than the rep quality behind it; a controlled set at RPE 7 beats a grinding set at RPE 9.5 every week of this phase. Gluteus maximus and gluteus medius volume is front-loaded because direct hip abduction work is the only reliable stimulus for the lateral hip — squats and hip thrusts alone do not train it. Strength on compounds should climb steadily for the first months; when 2 or more compounds stall for 3 sessions despite a deload, that is the signal to move to Phase 2 (Hypertrophy) with a 4-day upper/lower split.
