# COACH — Phase 0 Training Program (Gym Introduction)

2-week protocol for a complete beginner. Movement pattern learning, bodyweight or very light load, zero progressive overload, RPE 5-6 maximum. Source: knowledge/training-knowledge-base.md section 15 (Mersmann et al. 2017: tendon adaptation lags muscle adaptation; NSCA periodization literature: anatomical adaptation phase first).

## Session A — Monday

**Focus**: Full body A — glute activation and squat pattern

**Warmup**:
- 5 min light cardio (walk, bike, or elliptical)
- Bodyweight squats x 10 (slow, controlled)
- Glute bridges x 10 (feel the glutes, not the hamstrings)
- Banded lateral walks x 8/side

| Exercise                  | Sets | Reps    | Rest (s) | Target RPE | Progression  | Notes                                                        |
|---------------------------|------|---------|----------|------------|--------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| Bodyweight Glute Bridge   | 2    | 15      | 60       | 5          | form_mastery | Learn hip extension; squeeze glutes at top, not hamstrings   |
| Goblet Squat              | 2    | 12      | 90       | 6          | form_mastery | 0-8 kg. Depth to parallel, knee tracking, upright torso      |
| Band-Assisted Lat Pulldown| 2    | 12      | 60       | 5          | form_mastery | Light band. Feel the lats engage; retract shoulder blades    |
| Side-lying Hip Abduction  | 2    | 15/side | 60       | 6          | form_mastery | Slow and controlled; feel the outer hip (gluteus medius)     |
| Dead Bug                  | 2    | 8/side  | 60       | 5          | form_mastery | Bracing, breathing pattern, pelvic control                   |

## Session B — Wednesday

**Focus**: Full body B — hip hinge and pulling pattern

**Warmup**:
- 5 min light cardio (walk, bike, or elliptical)
- Hip hinge drill with broomstick x 10
- Bodyweight split squats x 6/leg
- Band pull-aparts x 12

|---------------------------|------|---------|----------|------------|--------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|

## Session C — Friday

**Focus**: Full body C — movement consolidation

**Warmup**:
- 5 min light cardio (walk, bike, or elliptical)
- Bodyweight squats to box x 8
- Single-leg glute bridges x 6/side
- Arm circles and scapular retractions x 10

## Progression Rules

- No progressive overload in Phase 0 — bodyweight or very light load only
- Progress movement quality: fuller range of motion, better control, cleaner tempo
- RPE 5-6 maximum on every set; end the set early if form degrades
- Learn the RPE scale from session 1 — rate every set in the training log

## Recovery Protocol

- At least one full rest day between sessions (Monday / Wednesday / Friday)
- Sleep 7-9 hours; recovery is where adaptation happens
- Walking on rest days is encouraged; no additional structured training
- Joint pain persisting beyond one week triggers a PHYSICIAN referral

## Phase Transition Criteria

- Bodyweight squat to parallel with good form x 15
- Glute bridge with clear glute activation (not hamstring dominant)
- Hip hinge with flat back (broomstick test)
- Split squat with stable balance
- No joint pain during any movement
- Minimum 6 sessions completed

## Session Notes

Phase 0 exists to prepare tendons and ligaments, groove movement patterns, and build gym confidence before any loading begins — connective tissue adapts more slowly than muscle, and skipping this step risks tendinopathy and poor motor patterns. Volumes are deliberately below the hypertrophy threshold: the goal these two weeks is pattern quality, not stimulus. For moderate gym anxiety: visit at off-peak hours, keep sessions under 45 minutes, and note that every movement here can be rehearsed at home first with no equipment. All three sessions repeat identically in week 2. When all phase transition criteria are met, COACH moves to Phase 1 (Foundation): 3x/week full body with initial progressive overload.
