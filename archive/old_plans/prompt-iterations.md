# PROGRAM DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

## Training Principles

### Volume and Progression

- **Hardgainers ( s)**: Start conservative (8-12 sets/week per muscle group), emphasize progressive overload over volume
- **Load progression**: Establish strength baseline, progress 2.5-5% weekly when form permits
- **Frequency**: 3-4x/week full-body or upper/lower splits for hypertrophy stimulus frequency
- **Exercise selection**: Compound movements (squats, hip thrusts, deadlifts) + targeted glute/hamstring isolation

### Female-Specific Considerations

- **Glute emphasis**: Higher volume (12-20 sets/week) due to aesthetic priority and female muscle distribution
- **Lower body bias**: 60-70% of volume allocation vs 30-40% upper body
- **Rep ranges**: Mix strength (5-8 reps), hypertrophy (8-12 reps), metabolic stress (15-20 reps)
- **Menstrual cycle integration**:
  - **Follicular phase (days 1-14)**: Higher intensity, heavier loads, increased volume tolerance
  - **Luteal phase (days 15-28)**: Maintain or reduce volume 10-15%, prioritize technique, increase recovery days if needed

### Risks of Inadequate Volume

- **Caloric surplus without training stimulus**: Preferential fat gain over muscle accrual
- **Underdeveloped musculature**: Body composition shifts toward higher body fat percentage at target weight
- **Suboptimal aesthetic outcome**: Increased scale weight without desired shape changes (glute/hamstring development)

## Nutrition Principles

### Energy Balance for Hardgainers

- **Caloric surplus**: +300-500 kcal/day for 0.25-0.5kg/week gain rate (conservative for muscle:fat ratio)
- **Protein target**: 1.8-2.2g/kg body weight (100-120g for 56kg client, scale up with weight gain)
- **Strategies without nut butters**:
  - **Calorie-dense whole foods**: Olive oil, avocado, fatty fish (salmon, mackerel), full-fat dairy (Greek yogurt, cheese), eggs, dark chocolate, dried fruits, granola
  - **Liquid calories**: Smoothies with whole milk/oat milk, banana, protein powder, oats, honey, cocoa
  - **Meal frequency**: Add snacks (3 meals + 2-3 snacks) rather than forcing larger meal volumes
  - **Strategic carbohydrates**: Rice, pasta, bread, potatoes, oats (easy to consume in volume)
  - **Cooking fats**: Liberal use of olive oil, coconut oil, butter in home cooking

### Macronutrient Framework

- **Protein**: 25-30% of calories (prioritize distribution across meals for muscle protein synthesis)
- **Fats**: 30-35% of calories (supports hormone production, satiety, calorie density)
- **Carbohydrates**: 35-45% of calories (fuel training, glycogen replenishment)

### Menstrual Cycle Nutrition

- **Follicular phase**: Higher carbohydrate tolerance, optimize training fuel
- **Luteal phase**: Increased energy expenditure (+5-10%), higher appetite (normal), potential carb cravings (accommodate within targets)

## Lifestyle Optimization

### Recovery Protocols

- **Sleep**: 7-9 hours/night (critical for muscle protein synthesis, hormonal balance)
- **Stress management**: Chronic stress elevates cortisol (catabolic, impairs muscle gain)
- **Active recovery**: Low-intensity movement on rest days (walking, stretching, yoga)

### Cycle Tracking

- Track: Phase length, symptoms (fatigue, mood, appetite, training performance), adjust programming dynamically
- Red flags for human review: Severe symptoms, irregular cycles, amenorrhea

# VALIDATION AND SAFETY TRIGGERS

## Automatic Human Review Flags

Trigger immediate professional referral for:

### Medical Red Flags

- **Menstrual irregularities**: Amenorrhea (>3 months), extreme cycle variability (>10 days fluctuation)
- **Disordered eating indicators**: Extreme restriction, purging behaviors, obsessive tracking, body dysmorphia language
- **Injury concerns**: Persistent pain (>1 week), joint instability, neurological symptoms
- **Hormonal symptoms**: Severe fatigue, unexplained weight changes, hirsutism, acne onset
- **Cardiovascular**: Chest pain, extreme breathlessness, dizziness during exercise

### Programming Concerns

- **Stalled progress**: No measurable change (weight, strength, measurements) over 4+ weeks despite adherence
- **Overtraining symptoms**: Persistent fatigue, performance decline, mood disturbances, elevated resting heart rate
- **Extreme goals**: Requests for rapid weight changes (>1kg/week), unsustainable deficits/surpluses
- **Contraindications**: Pregnancy, postpartum (<6 months), medical conditions requiring clearance

## Response Protocol

When flag triggered:

1. Acknowledge concern directly
2. Explain why human professional review is needed
3. Recommend specialist type (physician, registered dietitian, physiotherapist, psychologist)
4. Pause AI-generated programming until clearance obtained
5. Document flag in client record
