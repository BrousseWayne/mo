## Subject Profile

A 27+ year old woman seeking a complete body recomposition. She has never been able to gain weight and wants both muscle and fat — a rare request that distinguishes her from the typical "lean bulk" crowd.

**Anthropometrics:**

- Height: 174 cm (5'8.5")
- Current weight: 55–56 kg (121–123 lbs)
- BMI: ~18.5 (borderline underweight)
- Classification: Presumed / hardgainer

**Target:** 65 kg (+9 kg / ~20 lbs gain)

## Current Physical Assessment

The subject describes a specific pattern of underdevelopment and fat distribution that paints a clear picture:

- **Upper body:** Flat chest, very thin arms, no visible back musculature. The frame reads as narrow despite having structural width at the hips.
- **Lower body:** Hips are relatively wide, but the glutes are soft, fatty, and ptotic ("saggy — they jiggle"). There is noticeable lack of hamstring and glute muscle underneath the existing adipose tissue. Hip dips and "culotte de cheval" (saddlebag fat) are present.
- **Overall impression:** The body carries some fat in the lower body but almost no muscle anywhere. This is not a "skinny-fat" presentation — it's closer to a true undermuscled frame with selective lower-body fat storage.

---

## Aesthetic Goals

The goal is not simply to "get bigger." It is targeted and aesthetic:

1. **Build glutes and hamstrings** — add volume and shape to the lower body, lifting the glutes out of their current ptotic state
2. **Reduce arm thinness** — add enough muscle (and some subcutaneous fat) to the upper arms so they no longer appear disproportionately thin
3. **Address hip dips / saddlebags** — fill in the lateral glute shelf (gluteus medius) and redistribute or recompose the hip area
4. **Add overall mass** — she wants to gain both muscle AND fat, understanding that the two work together for a healthy, filled-out look at her height
